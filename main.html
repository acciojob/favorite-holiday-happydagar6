Paris, France


Paris, often referred to as the "City of Light," is a global center for art, fashion, gastronomy, and culture. Its 19th-century cityscape is crisscrossed by wide boulevards and the River Seine. Beyond such landmarks as the Eiffel Tower and the 12th-century Gothic Notre-Dame cathedral, the city is famous for its cafe culture and designer boutiques.

Top 5 Attractions

  1. The Eiffel Tower: The most iconic symbol of France, offering breathtaking views of the city.
  2. The Louvre Museum: Home to thousands of works of art, including the Mona Lisa and the Venus de Milo.
  3. Notre-Dame Cathedral: A masterpiece of French Gothic architecture located on the Île de la Cité.
  4. Montmartre: A historic hilltop district known for its artistic history and the white-domed Basilica of the Sacré-Cœur.
  5. Seine River Cruise: A boat tour that offers a unique perspective of the city's historic bridges and monuments.

Must-Try Foods

  • Croissants: Buttery, flaky pastries that are a staple of any French breakfast.
  • Macarons: Delicate meringue-based confections available in a rainbow of flavors.
  • Escargots de Bourgogne: Snails cooked in a delicious sauce of garlic butter and parsley.
  • Steak Frites: A simple yet classic dish consisting of steak paired with deep-fried potatoes.
  • Crêpes: Thin pancakes that can be served sweet with Nutella or savory with cheese and ham.
