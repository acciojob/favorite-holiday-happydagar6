Kyoto, Japan


Kyoto is widely considered the cultural heart of Japan, offering a stunning blend of ancient traditions and natural beauty. Known for its thousands of classical Buddhist temples, as well as gardens, imperial palaces, Shinto shrines, and traditional wooden houses, it is a destination that feels like stepping back in time. Whether you are viewing the cherry blossoms in spring or the vibrant maple leaves in autumn, the atmosphere is uniquely serene and captivating.

Top 5 Attractions

  1. Fushimi Inari Taisha: Famous for its thousands of vermilion torii gates, which straddle a network of trails behind its main buildings.
  2. Kinkaku-ji (The Golden Pavilion): A Zen temple whose top two floors are completely covered in gold leaf.
  3. Arashiyama Bamboo Grove: A mesmerizing natural forest where towering bamboo stalks create a unique and otherworldly atmosphere.
  4. Kiyomizu-dera: An iconic wooden temple offering a spectacular view of the city, especially during cherry blossom season.
  5. Gion District: The most famous geisha district in Kyoto, known for its preserved machiya (wooden merchant houses).

Must-Try Foods

  • Kaiseki Ryori: A traditional multi-course Japanese dinner that represents the pinnacle of Japanese haute cuisine.
  • Yudofu (Tofu Hot Pot): A simple yet delicious dish made with tofu simmered in a kelp-based broth, a local specialty.
  • Matcha Sweets: From ice cream to cakes, the high-quality green tea grown in the nearby Uji region is a staple ingredient.
  • Shojin Ryori: Traditional Buddhist vegetarian cuisine often served at temples.
